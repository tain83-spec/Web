Therapist Responsibilities

As your therapist, I commit to:

  • Be available at the agreed time and start and end sessions punctually
  • Provide a quiet, private, and undisturbed space for our sessions
  • Maintain safe and professional boundaries at all times
  • Treat all contact and information as strictly confidential
  • Encourage your autonomy and work collaboratively with you
  • Work within the UKCP Code of Ethics and Professional Practice
  • Attend regular clinical supervision to monitor and develop my practice
  • Review our therapeutic work and relationship with you regularly
  • If I need to cancel a session, offer an alternative appointment as soon as possible
Client Responsibilities
Attendance & Punctuality
  • Attend sessions punctually at the agreed time
  • I will hold the session open for 10 minutes. If you have not arrived and I have not heard from you, the session will be considered cancelled
Cancellations
  • Give a minimum of 4 full working days' notice (Monday–Friday) when cancelling or changing an appointment
  • Where possible, one alternative time or day will be offered within the same week for sessions cancelled with sufficient notice
  • Refunds are only offered where the required notice period has been given
Payments
  • Payments are made in advance of sessions on a fortnightly or monthly basis as agreed
Communication
  • Contact outside of sessions should be limited to making, changing, or cancelling appointments, unless otherwise agreed
  • Be respectful to the therapist and their property at all times
Safety & Risk
  • Agree to allow me to contact your GP if there is a serious concern about risk to yourself or others
  • Inform me if you are in, or considering entering into, any other therapeutic relationship
About Your Therapist

I am a UKCP-registered Psychotherapeutic Counsellor, trained through the Psychotherapeutic Counselling and Intersubjective Psychotherapy College (PCIPC). My approach is relational and trauma-informed, attending to mind, body, and soul in the context of your circumstances and where you are in your life.

Confidentiality

Everything discussed in our sessions is treated as strictly confidential in line with the UKCP Code of Ethics and Professional Practice (clauses 18–22).

Legal and Ethical Limits
  • Risk of serious harm — where you or another person appear to be at serious risk
  • Safeguarding — where there is concern about harm or neglect to a child or vulnerable adult
  • Legal obligation — where required within judicial or administrative proceedings
  • Terrorism or money laundering — where required by law
Data Protection
  • Your personal information is stored securely in line with UK GDPR
  • Used only for the purposes of providing your therapeutic care
  • Retained in line with professional guidance, then securely destroyed
  • You have the right to request access to information held about you
Supervision

I attend regular clinical supervision to maintain the quality of your care. Aspects of our work may be discussed. Your name and any identifying information will never be shared. My supervisor is bound by the same confidentiality commitment.

Records of Sessions

I do not routinely keep notes of our sessions. The only documents I retain are appointment records and this signed contract.

  • If you would like me to keep session notes, please let me know explicitly
  • A thematic outline of sessions can be arranged at an additional charge of £10 per summary
Online Sessions
  • You are responsible for ensuring you are in a private, quiet, and confidential space
  • Please ensure a stable internet connection and that your device is charged
  • Sessions will not proceed if a third party can hear or observe without prior agreement
  • If the connection fails, I will attempt to contact you by phone
Crisis and Emergency Arrangements

I am not an emergency service and cannot guarantee availability between sessions. If you are in crisis outside of session times, please contact:

Samaritans — 116 123 (free, 24 hours, 7 days a week)

Crisis text line — Text SHOUT to 85258

NHS 111 — 111

Emergency services — 999

Scope and Limitations

This service does not provide emergency or crisis intervention, psychiatric assessment or diagnosis, medical or prescribing advice, or legal advice.

Holidays & Planned Absences
  • A maximum of 2 consecutive sessions may be missed
  • A minimum of 10 working days' notice is required if 1–2 consecutive sessions will be missed
  • Where possible, alternative appointment times will be negotiated
Ending Therapy

Endings are a significant part of the therapeutic process. A closing process of 2–3 sessions is recommended once an ending has been agreed.

Complaints Procedure

If you have concerns, please raise these with me directly in the first instance. If unresolved, you may contact the UKCP:

  • Website: www.psychotherapy.org.uk
  • Email: complaints@ukcp.org.uk
  • Telephone: 020 7014 9955
Changes to this Contract

Any changes will be made with a minimum of 10 working days' notice by either party. This contract is subject to regular review.
